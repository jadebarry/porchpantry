Frequently Asked Questions

What is The Porch Pantry?
The Porch Pantry is a community-led emergency food relief initiative serving Columbus, Ohio and surrounding areas. We connect volunteers who can deliver food with families in need, operating on a one-day-at-a-time basis to provide immediate assistance.
How does the food delivery process work?
Families in need submit a request through our form. Volunteers sign up to deliver food on specific days. We match volunteers with families based on location and availability. Deliveries are contactless and left on the porch to ensure privacy and dignity for recipients.
Who can request food assistance?
Anyone in Columbus, Ohio or surrounding areas experiencing food insecurity can request assistance. There are no income requirements or qualifications needed. We believe everyone deserves access to food in times of need.
How can I volunteer as a food deliverer?
Simply click the "Sign Up to Deliver Food" button and fill out the form. You can choose which days work for your schedule. We'll send you details about the delivery, including the family's address and any dietary needs or preferences.
What kind of food should volunteers deliver?
Volunteers typically provide grocery items, prepared meals, or a combination of both. We appreciate deliveries that include a variety of nutritious options such as fresh produce, proteins, grains, and shelf-stable items. Any dietary restrictions will be noted in your delivery assignment.
Is my donation tax-deductible?
Currently, The Porch Pantry operates as a grassroots community initiative. We are working toward establishing formal nonprofit status. Please consult with your tax advisor regarding the deductibility of your donation.
How is my privacy protected if I request food?
We take privacy seriously. Your information is only shared with the volunteer making your delivery. All deliveries are contactless and left on your porch. Volunteers are committed to treating all recipients with respect and dignity.
Can I request food assistance more than once?
Yes. We understand that food insecurity can be an ongoing challenge. You're welcome to submit new requests as needed. Our goal is to provide support during difficult times, however long they may last.
